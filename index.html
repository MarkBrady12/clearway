The Problem

Roads weren't built
for this.

When 40,000 people leave a stadium simultaneously, every one of them tries to drive home at the same moment. Road networks are designed for steady flow — not spikes. The result is gridlock within minutes of the final whistle.

Traffic management strategies are near-impossible to test in real conditions. Too disruptive. Too expensive. Too slow to iterate. Until now.

How It Works

Incentivise the wait.
Flatten the spike.

Waves is a staggered departure management system. Attendees choose a departure window at booking. Those who wait longer receive a greater reward. The road network gets the time it needs to recover.

01 — BOOK

Choose at purchase

When buying a ticket, attendees select a departure window — immediately, +15, +30, or +45 minutes. Each window carries a different incentive automatically optimised by our pricing engine per event.

02 — ATTEND

Nothing changes

The event runs as normal. A push notification reminds attendees of their chosen window as the event ends. Those in later waves get a cue when it's their time to leave.

03 — DEPART

Verify and claim

At departure time, attendees verify their location via geolocation to unlock their reward — a venue food voucher or a partial ticket refund sent instantly to their account.

ClearWay CitySimulator

We didn't assume.
We simulated.

A custom-built cell-based city traffic simulator — junctions, A* routing, adaptive signal control, event injection pipelines. Purpose-built to answer one question: do waves work at city scale?

01

Wave gap is everything. Release below the network drain time (~23 ticks) and congestion worsens relative to instant release. Waves need space to clear before the next one arrives.

02

Most effective at 500–1,600 attendees — the range covering the majority of real-world venues. Above that, infrastructure becomes the binding constraint, not strategy.

03

Adaptive signals amplify the benefit. Combining wave release with adaptive traffic lights outperforms either intervention alone — the effects compound.

04

No infrastructure required. The congestion benefit comes entirely from the release schedule. Deployable at any venue, immediately.
